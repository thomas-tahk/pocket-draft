# SWUCUBE — Pocket Cube 2 Player Draft Rules

> Source: SWUCUBE ruleset shared via YouTube video. Captured as-is for reference.

## The Goal

Players draft to build their **COLLECTION** via a 10 round grid draft and augment their **COLLECTION** after the draft via effect cards and by acquiring cards from the **SHOP**.
Players then build 3 distinct decks using cards in their **COLLECTION**.
The match is a conquest format — to win the match you need to win at least 1 game with each of your decks.

---

## Draft Pool Preparation

- Entire 55 card **TRAINER DRAFT POOL**
- 30 random Pokemon dealt from the **POKEMON DRAFT POOL** (of the 151 available)
- 5 draft effect cards (3x WONDER PICK, 1x BOOSTER PACK, 1x TRADE)

Shuffle into a single stack of 90 cards.

---

## The Draft

Deal 9 cards from the top of the DRAFT POOL in a 3x3 grid. The player determined to be going first selects a 3 card row or column.

The second player selects a row or column of the remaining 6 cards (either 2 or 3 cards). Remaining cards are set aside.

Rinse/repeat 9 more times until the DRAFT POOL is fully exhausted, alternating which player picks first each round.

After the draft is complete, undrafted Pokemon and Trainer cards are returned to their respective DRAFT POOLs before the next step.

---

## Shop Phase

Each player receives X **SHOP TICKETS** (suggested: 5, YMMV).

The player that went second in the draft gets the first chance to use a draft effect card. This is important because each Pokemon only appears once in the DRAFT POOL. When using the draft effect cards players have a chance to select Pokemon to add to their COLLECTION.

Once all draft effects are used, players may take turns spending SHOP TICKETS. There is no competition for resources here so turn order doesn't matter. Shop transactions are public information, just like the draft.

### Shop Rates

- **Same Name Pokemon** (exactly the same name) from the Shop are free to interchange. If you have Genetic Apex Zebstrika in your COLLECTION you get free access to alternate versions of Zebstrika from the shop.
- **Different Name but Same Type Pokemon** (e.g. you draft Charizard Ex but want Charizard Mega Ex) cost 1 Shop Ticket to access. You get access to 2 copies of the alt for 1 Ticket. Basically, if the extra Pokemon gives you access to a 3rd–4th copy of the same type of Pokemon, it costs a ticket.
- **Pokemon specific Trainers** (e.g. Brock, Blaine, Lusamine) cost 1 Ticket per playset.
- **Arceus** and **Eevee** are stored separate from the draft box.
  - Arceus ex costs 1 ticket; regular Arceus is free. (Arceus ex and Arceus are not in the draft pool and can only be accessed if a player drafts one of the Pokemon with an Arceus ability.)
  - Eevee Pokemon are freely available in any combination to players that drafted Eevelutions during the draft. Even Eevee ex is free.

> **Note:** The Shop contains extra copies of all trainers in the DRAFT POOL so that between the DRAFT POOL and the SHOP there are 2x copies of each available Trainer for each player. If the SHOP is exhausted of a particular card but a copy was undrafted, players may access those copies with Shop Tickets as if they were in the Shop. (This is just a space saving measure.)

> **Important Rule:** Players are limited to 2x of each Trainer card (excluding Professor's Research and Pokeball) across their entire collection.

> **Wisdom from SWUCUBE:** Look at rounding out your Supporters and Items from the shop first. You don't want to go into a match with no copies of Cyrus/Sabrina/Repel.

---

## Deckbuilding

Once draft effects and shop tickets are spent, players commence building their decks.

During deckbuilding players may access under-evolutions and second copies of Pokemon in their COLLECTION for free. These are stored outside the DRAFT POOL to avoid confusion.

General deckbuilding rules from Pocket apply.

Players get access to as many copies of **Pokeball** and **Prof's Research** as they want, for free.

Players also get access to **2x copies of X Speed, Potion, and Red Card** for free.

Players must build **3 distinct decks**. Once decks are built there is no sideboarding until after the match. If you want to rebuild and do another match, go for it.

> **Wisdom from SWUCUBE:** Try and build a well rounded set of decks that cover each other's weaknesses to typing and speed of play. You don't want three Fire decks or three decks that all fold to an early Farfetch'd smacking for 40 repeatedly.

---

## Gameplay

Games are played until a player wins once with each deck. Before each game players secretly select a deck and then reveal their choices simultaneously.

---

## Other Notes

- **Energy Tracking:** Use colored dice. In the event a player builds a 2 color deck they roll dice for RNG to determine which energy is set for next turn. Alternatively, shuffle a small deck of traditional Pokemon TCG energy cards.
- **Pull from Deck Effects:** No reliable way to do this IRL without an honor system. Preferred method: look at cards from the top of your deck one at a time until you hit the relevant card type, then shuffle once you obtain the needed card.
- **Random Target Effects** (e.g. Genetic Apex Dragonite, Magcargo, Rayquaza ex): Use dice.
- **Organization:** A simple sticker system works to differentiate DRAFT POOL cards from non-pool cards. Use a different colored sticker on DRAFT POOL cards that have alt versions available in the SHOP.

---

## Expanding the Cube for More Players

151 Pokemon lines are more than sufficient to support 8 or even more players, but the limiting factor is trainer cards. For more players, print up more trainer proxies. You probably don't need a full 2x of EVERY trainer for each player but you definitely want it for the most desirable effects (e.g. Cyrus, Sabrina, Copycat).

For more than 2 players, a traditional booster draft structure is suggested (proportionally expanding the 2-player draft pool). Not yet playtested at higher player counts.

---

## Playing with Young Kids

Pokemon Pocket is an ideal learner TCG. Ignore all the draft effects and shop tickets — it's basically just open season. Build fun decks they're interested in, intentionally underpower your deck, and play as many games as you have time for.
